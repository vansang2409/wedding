# Storyboard: Lavender Dream Wedding Journey

## Director's Note

This storyboard follows the frozen Creative Direction in `creative-direction-lavender-dream.md`.

The website is treated as a short interactive film. The guest is not browsing a page; they are entering a lavender dream and moving through a sequence of emotional spaces.

The camera is guided by scroll. Each scene should feel connected to the previous one through light, particles, floating objects, and shared visual rhythm.

The emotional arc:

```txt
Arrival
→ Memory
→ Joy
→ Ceremony
→ Anticipation
→ Response
→ Gratitude
```

The visual arc:

```txt
Lavender dream haze
→ warm memory path
→ floating photo field
→ champagne glass cards
→ gold countdown halo
→ quiet RSVP chamber
→ ivory ending glow
```

## Complete User Journey

The guest enters the website and first sees a quiet loading moment: a warm ivory screen, a small mark, and a subtle sense of anticipation. Nothing feels rushed. The experience opens like the first frame of a film.

The loading fades, and the guest arrives in a dream-like space. Văn Sang and Thu Thương are introduced with large cinematic text. Their main visual floats beside warm geometric forms and soft petals. The scene says, without over-explaining: this is their world, and the guest is invited inside.

As the guest scrolls, the opening scene does not end abruptly. It drifts backward. The golden forms and floating particles become a path. The guest moves into the couple's story. Memory cards appear along a soft timeline, each one carrying a moment. The camera glides past them as if walking through suspended memories.

The story path dissolves into photographs. The guest enters a wider, airier space where photos float naturally at different depths. The gallery is not a grid; it feels like memories caught in a soft lavender atmosphere.

The photos drift away and the scene becomes calmer. Glass cards assemble in the center. The wedding information appears: date, time, venue, address. The tone becomes clearer and ceremonial. Beauty gives way to practical clarity without breaking the dream.

The wedding date becomes the emotional center of the next scene. A halo forms. The countdown appears. The camera slows. This is the pause before the day itself, a quiet moment of anticipation.

The halo softens into a glass chamber. The RSVP form rises into view. The motion becomes minimal so the guest can focus. This is where emotion becomes response: will they join the celebration?

Finally, the form dissolves into light. The guest reaches the ending: a final thank-you message, the couple's names, and a soft ivory glow. Particles drift upward and fade. The experience rests, like the final shot of a film.

## Scene 1: Opening

### Scene Objective

Introduce the couple and establish the Lavender Dream world. This scene must immediately communicate that the site is not a normal invitation page. It is an emotional cinematic journey.

The opening should answer:

```txt
Who are they?
What is the feeling?
Why should I continue scrolling?
```

### Emotional Feeling

- Arrival.
- Wonder.
- Warm anticipation.
- A private invitation into the couple's dream space.

The guest should feel gently pulled in, not shouted at.

### Camera Movement

The camera begins still, as if hovering in a soft room.

Shooting direction:

```txt
Frame 1:
Wide dream space. Text on the left. Couple visual on the right.

Camera action:
Slow dolly-in.

Secondary movement:
Tiny horizontal drift following pointer on desktop.
No pointer movement on mobile.
```

Camera progression:

```txt
Start:
Camera is slightly far, calm, centered.

Middle:
Camera pushes in toward the couple visual and text.

End:
Camera begins to drift past the opening objects, preparing to enter the memory path.
```

### Lighting

Lighting is bright and soft.

Key light:

- Top-right.
- Warm champagne.

Fill:

- Ivory.
- Very soft, almost paper-like.

Atmosphere:

- Lavender haze around the edges.
- Gold glow behind the hero visual.

No harsh contrast. The scene should feel like morning light through a dream.

### Background

Background layers:

```txt
Layer 1:
Warm ivory base.

Layer 2:
Lavender haze in the far background.

Layer 3:
Champagne/gold radial glow near the couple visual.

Layer 4:
Tiny dust particles, very sparse.
```

The background must leave enough empty space around the headline. Negative space is part of the premium feeling.

### Foreground Objects

Main objects:

- Large cinematic headline.
- Save The Date label.
- Couple visual.
- Date pill.
- Wedding day pill.
- Primary CTA.
- Soft geometric gold surface.
- Small floating decorative objects.
- Sparse blush petals.

Object behavior:

- Couple visual floats gently.
- Gold surface sits behind the couple visual like a dream platform.
- Petals drift diagonally.
- Date chip feels like a physical label attached to the scene.

### Scroll Progression

Estimated scene range:

```txt
0% - 15% of total scroll
```

Within scene:

```txt
0% - 20%:
Loading has ended. Text and hero objects settle.

20% - 65%:
Camera slowly pushes inward. Objects breathe subtly.

65% - 100%:
Opening text begins to lose dominance. Visual objects drift backward. The story path begins to form.
```

### User Interaction

Desktop:

- Pointer movement creates very subtle parallax.
- CTA lifts on hover.
- Floating photo/object layers respond slightly.

Mobile:

- No pointer parallax.
- CTA scrolls to Scene 2.
- Visual objects are simplified and stacked lower in the viewport.

Audio:

- Sound control is visible.
- Audio only starts by user action.

### Animation Sequence

Shot sequence:

```txt
1. Loading fades out.
2. Save The Date label appears with a slight paper tilt.
3. Headline lines reveal one by one.
4. Description fades in.
5. Date and wedding pills settle.
6. CTA appears.
7. Couple visual rises softly into place.
8. Particles and petals begin slow drift.
```

Timing:

- Label: `600ms - 720ms`
- Headline lines: `700ms - 900ms`, staggered
- Couple visual: `900ms - 1100ms`
- Ambient loop: `8s - 14s`

### Transition Into Next Scene

Opening becomes Story.

Transition direction:

```txt
The gold surface stretches visually into a path.
Petals become memory particles.
Small floating objects drift backward.
The scene warms slightly.
Timeline markers begin to appear from depth.
```

There should be no hard cut. The guest should feel they are passing through the opening environment into a corridor of memories.

### Estimated Scroll Duration

Desktop:

- `100vh - 120vh`

Tablet:

- `100vh - 115vh`

Mobile:

- `110vh - 135vh`, because content stacks vertically.

### Mobile Adaptation

Mobile framing:

```txt
Top:
Logo and controls.

Middle:
Large headline and CTA.

Lower:
Couple visual partially visible to encourage scroll.
```

Effects:

- Fewer particles.
- No pointer parallax.
- Reduced blur.
- Couple visual appears lower and larger.

The opening must still feel cinematic, not like a simple mobile hero.

## Scene 2: Our Story

### Scene Objective

Show the relationship as a sequence of memories. The goal is not to explain every detail but to suggest emotional progression.

This scene should make the guest feel:

```txt
They have history.
They have grown together.
This wedding is the result of a story.
```

### Emotional Feeling

- Nostalgia.
- Warmth.
- Intimacy.
- Gentle memory.

This is quieter than the opening. The camera becomes more observant.

### Camera Movement

The camera glides along a memory path.

Shooting direction:

```txt
Frame:
Story title on one side.
Memory cards suspended along a curved path.

Camera action:
Slow lateral glide through the cards.

Depth:
Active card is closest.
Previous and next cards sit slightly farther away.
```

The camera should feel like walking past physical memory objects.

### Lighting

Lighting becomes warmer and more nostalgic.

Key:

- Soft amber.

Fill:

- Cream.

Atmospheric tint:

- Lavender reduced.
- Sepia warmth increases.

Active card:

- Slightly brighter edge.
- More readable.

Inactive cards:

- Slightly softer.
- Less contrast.

### Background

Background layers:

```txt
Layer 1:
Warm cream.

Layer 2:
Soft sepia gradient.

Layer 3:
Thin timeline path.

Layer 4:
Small gold dust.

Layer 5:
Blurred transition lights.
```

The timeline should feel like a path of memory, not a corporate process timeline.

### Foreground Objects

Objects:

- Scene title.
- Timeline path.
- Date markers.
- Memory cards.
- Small image fragments.
- Warm light blooms.

Memory card content:

- Date or moment label.
- Photo.
- Short title.
- Short emotional description.

Cards should feel like paper/photo objects suspended in depth.

### Scroll Progression

Estimated scene range:

```txt
15% - 35% of total scroll
```

Within scene:

```txt
0% - 20%:
Timeline path appears.
First memory card becomes active.

20% - 50%:
Camera glides toward second memory.
First card drifts back.

50% - 80%:
Third memory becomes active.
Timeline glow travels forward.

80% - 100%:
Cards begin to loosen from the path.
They prepare to become floating gallery photos.
```

### User Interaction

Desktop:

- Hovering a memory card brings it slightly forward.
- Card shadow deepens.
- Image gently scales.

Mobile:

- Cards stack vertically.
- No hover dependency.
- Each card is large and readable.

### Animation Sequence

Shot sequence:

```txt
1. Story title fades in.
2. Timeline path draws softly.
3. First memory card rises from depth.
4. Date marker glows.
5. Next cards appear at different depths.
6. As the user scrolls, active emphasis shifts.
```

Timing:

- Path draw: `900ms - 1400ms`
- Card reveal: `700ms - 1100ms`
- Active marker glow: ambient loop `4s - 6s`

### Transition Into Next Scene

Story becomes Gallery.

Transition direction:

```txt
Timeline line dissolves into light trails.
Memory cards detach from the path.
Photos expand outward.
Background brightens from warm memory to airy ivory.
Lavender haze returns softly.
```

The user should feel memories are opening into a wider photo space.

### Estimated Scroll Duration

Desktop:

- `130vh - 160vh`

Tablet:

- `120vh - 145vh`

Mobile:

- Natural stacked length, approximately `180vh - 240vh`

### Mobile Adaptation

Mobile version:

- Vertical timeline.
- Large cards.
- Clear text.
- Minimal depth.
- Light animation only.

The story should feel like a cinematic scroll journal on mobile.

## Scene 3: Gallery

### Scene Objective

Show photos as memories floating in a dream space. Avoid a grid. The gallery should feel discovered rather than browsed.

### Emotional Feeling

- Joy.
- Airiness.
- Wonder.
- Lightness.

The scene should feel like the story has opened into a room full of memories.

### Camera Movement

The camera pulls back and enters a wider space.

Shooting direction:

```txt
Frame:
Photos floating at different depths.
Some photos near the camera.
Some photos farther away.

Camera action:
Slow drift through the photo field.

Movement:
Diagonal, not purely vertical.
```

Desktop should feel spatial. Mobile should feel like a controlled vertical photo stack.

### Lighting

Lighting becomes brighter again.

Key:

- Ivory front light.

Accent:

- Lavender haze in distance.
- Champagne edge light on photo frames.

Shadows:

- Soft under each photo.
- No harsh contrast.

### Background

Background layers:

```txt
Layer 1:
Open ivory field.

Layer 2:
Pale lavender mist.

Layer 3:
Subtle horizontal/diagonal light trail.

Layer 4:
Tiny dust and petals.
```

The background should feel spacious enough for photos to breathe.

### Foreground Objects

Objects:

- Floating photos.
- Feature couple photo.
- Smaller candid photos.
- Thin light trail.
- Sparse particles.

Photo behavior:

- Different sizes.
- Different rotations.
- Different depths.
- Some partial offscreen framing is allowed on desktop.

### Scroll Progression

Estimated scene range:

```txt
35% - 55% of total scroll
```

Within scene:

```txt
0% - 20%:
Photos appear from memory cards.

20% - 70%:
Camera passes through the photo field.
Feature photo becomes dominant.

70% - 100%:
Photos drift outward.
Center space clears for wedding information.
```

### User Interaction

Desktop:

- Hover photo to bring it forward.
- Slight scale increase.
- Shadow deepens.
- Optional focused overlay in later implementation.

Mobile:

- Tap can expand a photo in a later module.
- Initial implementation may keep a simple stack.

### Animation Sequence

Shot sequence:

```txt
1. First large photo emerges.
2. Smaller photos float in from depth.
3. Photo field stabilizes.
4. Active photos drift subtly.
5. Outgoing photos move toward the edges.
```

Timing:

- Photo reveal: `700ms - 1000ms`
- Photo stagger: `80ms - 120ms`
- Ambient drift: `8s - 16s`

### Transition Into Next Scene

Gallery becomes Wedding Information.

Transition direction:

```txt
Photos separate toward the edges.
The center becomes calmer.
Glass cards begin forming.
Lavender haze lowers.
Champagne lighting increases.
```

The mood changes from joyful exploration to elegant clarity.

### Estimated Scroll Duration

Desktop:

- `130vh - 160vh`

Tablet:

- `110vh - 140vh`

Mobile:

- `180vh - 240vh`, depending on number of photos shown.

### Mobile Adaptation

Mobile version:

- Large stacked photos.
- Slight rotation.
- No dense overlap.
- No hover interaction.
- Fewer particles.

The user should still feel that photos are floating, even though the layout is simpler.

This scene must be beautiful, but readability wins.

- Ceremony.
- Clarity.
- Elegance.
- Calm confidence.

The guest should think: "I know when and where to go."

### Camera Movement

The camera slows and centers.

Shooting direction:

```txt

Scene title on one side.
Floating glass cards on the other.

Camera action:
Subtle forward movement.
Very slight orbit on desktop.

Stability:
Higher than previous scenes.
```

### Lighting

Lighting becomes champagne and controlled.

Key:

- Warm champagne from top-right.

Fill:

- Soft ivory.

Glass edge:

- White highlight.

Accent:

- Orange for icons and important actions.

### Background

Background layers:

```txt
Layer 1:
Cream/champagne base.

Layer 2:
Subtle lavender haze behind cards.

Layer 3:
Thin SVG constellation lines.

Layer 4:
Small gold dots.
```

### Foreground Objects

- Time card.
- Venue card.
- Address/map card.
- Thin connecting SVG lines.
- Small icon circles.

Card behavior:

- Main date card is dominant.

- Map action is visible but not visually louder than the date.

### Scroll Progression

Estimated scene range:

```txt
55% - 70% of total scroll
```

Within scene:

```txt
0% - 25%:

Glass cards begin to assemble.

25% - 65%:
```

### User Interaction

Desktop:

- Edge highlight increases.

Mobile:

- Cards stack vertically.
- Large tap targets.
- Minimal hover-like effects.

### Animation Sequence

Shot sequence:

```txt
1. Main date card fades in first.
2. Time and venue cards rise in.
3. Address card appears last.
4. Constellation lines draw softly behind them.
5. Cards hover very subtly.
```

Timing:

- Main card reveal: `800ms`

### Transition Into Next Scene

### Estimated Scroll Duration

Desktop:

- `100vh - 130vh`

Tablet:

- `100vh - 120vh`

Mobile:

- No large 3D tilt.

- Text remains fully readable.

- A ceremonial pause.

### Camera Movement


This should be one of the calmest scenes.

- Countdown digits.

## Continuity Rules

The scenes must never feel like unrelated blocks. Use these continuity rules during implementation:

1. Opening gold shape becomes the memory path.
2. Story cards become gallery photo planes.
3. Gallery photos drift outward to reveal information cards.
4. Wedding date card becomes countdown halo.
5. Countdown halo becomes RSVP glass backlight.
6. RSVP glass dissolves into Thank You glow.
7. Lavender atmosphere appears throughout, but changes intensity by scene.
8. Orange remains the active interaction color.
9. Particles support transitions but never dominate.
10. Mobile keeps the same emotional sequence with simpler motion.

## Final Shot

The final impression should be:

```txt
I did not just read an invitation.
I moved through their story.
```
